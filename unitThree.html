Real Gross Domestic Product: Frequency [Quarterly]

Real GDP measures the total output of goods and services in the economy. In the AD-AS model, changes in aggregate demand shift output and influence national income levels. This graph helps visualize those fluctuations over time.

Real Gross Private Domestic Investment: Frequency [Quarterly]

Real private domestic investment is a component of aggregate demand and tends to rise during expansions and fall during recessions. It reflects business confidence and interest rate sensitivity, making it a core factor in short-run economic fluctuations and AD shifts.

Real Net Exports of Goods and Services: Frequency [Quarterly]

Net exports are calculated as exports minus imports and represent the international component of aggregate demand. Changes in trade balances directly influence GDP and shift the AD curve in the AD-AS model.

Shares of GDP - Net Exports: Frequency [Quarterly]

This graph shows net exports as a share of GDP, helping visualize how international trade contributes to total economic output. It's useful for analyzing the relative importance of trade in shifting national equilibrium and influencing aggregate demand.

Median Consumer Price Index: Frequency [Monthly]

The median CPI offers a smoother measure of inflation by filtering out extreme price changes. It's a useful alternative to headline CPI when examining underlying inflation trends caused by changes in aggregate demand or supply.

Industrial Production: Frequency [Monthly]

Industrial production tracks the real output of factories, mines, and utilities, and is a solid proxy for the supply side of the economy. Changes here can reflect shifts in short-run aggregate supply and the economy’s productive capacity.
