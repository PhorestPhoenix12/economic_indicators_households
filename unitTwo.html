Unemployment Rate: Frequency [Monthly]

The unemployment rate measures the percentage of the labor force that is jobless and actively seeking work, illustrating one of the three key economic indicators and how labor market conditions change across the business cycle.

Consumer Price Index for Urban Consumers: Frequency [Monthly]

This graph tracks changes in the price level over time, allowing us to measure inflation and evaluate the purchasing power of money, another essential macroeconomic indicator.

Real Disposable Personal Income: Frequency [Monthly]

Real disposable income represents the after-tax income households have to spend or save, helping to analyze changes in consumer demand and living standards during different phases of the business cycle.

GDP Price Deflator: Frequency [Quarterly]

The GDP deflator is a broad measure of inflation across the entire economy and is used to convert nominal GDP into real GDP, allowing economists to separate changes in output from changes in price level.

Labor Force Participation Rate: Frequency [Monthly]

This graph shows the percentage of working-age people who are either employed or actively seeking work, helping economists assess the true strength of the labor market beyond just the unemployment rate.

Real Gross Domestic Product: Frequency [Quarterly]

This graph shows the inflation-adjusted value of all goods and services produced in the U.S. over time, helping visualize the economy’s output capacity — a concept tied to scarcity, choice, and production possibilities.

Real Potential GDP: Frequency [Quarterly]

Real potential GDP represents the economy's maximum sustainable output using existing resources and technology, reflecting the idea of efficiency and the production possibilities curve (PPC) frontier.

Real Gross Private Domestic Investment: Frequency [Quarterly]

Investment spending reflects how firms allocate resources to capital goods, showing trade-offs between current consumption and future production possibilities.

Real Exports of Goods and Services: Frequency [Yearly]

Exports illustrate the benefits of specialization and comparative advantage — nations trade to obtain goods they produce less efficiently, which increases total output globally.

Real Imports of Goods and Services: Frequency [Yearly]

Imports reflect opportunity cost — countries choose to import goods that others produce at lower relative costs, improving overall efficiency through trade.
